What is Animation?

Animation had gone through a drastic change throughout the courses of time. Starting out as simply hand drawn images, to now with much smoother animations with the assistance of technology but also as people continue to refine the skill, and maybe in the future where AI can essentially become an animator themselves.

As for introducing animations, the easiest way to describe animation would be many individual drawings put together to create the illusion of movement. Such movements could range from simply a single character walking, slight hand movements and such to complex movements such as full on dancing, and animating multiple character movements in backgrounds. There are also many types of styles for animations, ranging from 2D, 3D, to stop motion animation and so much more. This website would mainly focus on the 2D animations but it might include other types of animation styles throughout.

Current Technology

Beginners

Pencil2D is great for those that want to experience 2d hand-drawn animations and great for those just entered the field of animations with it not having so many robust features that may confuse you and it being free to use.

For your stylus pen any affordable one will most likely work but we mainly recommend Adonit Dash 2 a pen that is affordable but with the downside of not having some of the features a more expensive pen would, still a great stylus pen for those wishing to test the waters with animations in general.

Now lastly for beginners is the trusty device you will be sketching out all your animations on. We would recommend you to use any device you own to start out with as to not spend any on something that might later just turn into a hobby. As long as that device is compatible with such animation software and pen you will be good to go, but mainly you would look for a tablet when you want to animate.

Intermediate

Now another free software for those who are not beginning out, but also not sure if they want to take on animation full time Is Blender, it is a great software that will let you experience rigging, modeling, motion tracking, rendering and more. It is great for those that may not be ready for the more complex and advanced animation softwares out there.

A good stylus pen for those that want their budget to be within 3 digits would be the Wacom KP504E Pro Pen 2 that allows for 60 levels of tilt recognition, 8192 levels of pressure sensitivity, virtual lag free tracking and much more. Overall it is a great stylus for those that want a pen within 3 digits.

A great tablet for those who wish for a moderate price is the Kamvas Pro 16 with its large screen,high resolution and with it being able to be easily carried around. Overall a great tablet for those that have interest in having animation play a part in their life.

Future Technology

AI Copying Animators Style(ACAS)

ACAS, a AI that has been given the ability to analyze an animator's artstyle and perfectly replicate it through the given samples so that it can lessen the stress of the animators due to being given short deadlines which leads to increased work time. ACAS would soon be the future of the animation industry and will help many that are involved in this field of work. ACAS is built with the purpose of recreating a artstyle in mind and not improving upon it. So ACAS gives the impression of a clone of the original animator which would further decrease the stress of the animator that comes from them worrying over the fact that the AI might drastically change the style of animation, which would lead to a major mix up. Overall ACAS would be the #1 support for animators in the soon to be future. Furthermore, if requested to demonstrate an artstyle of another animator for inspiration, ACAS asks for permission from the original creator first and if approved ACAS would show one of its projects that it had worked on alongside said creator.
